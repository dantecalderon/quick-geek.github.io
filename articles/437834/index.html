‚ÄúVirtual IT Robbery Event‚Äù or how was the RuCTF 2012 final held
From April 19 to April 22, 2012, the final of the All-Russian inter-university information protection competition RuCTF 2012 was held in UrFU (Yekaterinburg).

RuCTF is an all-Russian inter-university information protection competition that has been held since 2008 on the principles of the CTF game: at the beginning of the game, teams receive identical servers with a predefined set of vulnerable services, and the participants' task is to find vulnerabilities, close them and use them to private information (flags) from rivals.


The RuCTF is organized by the Institute of Mathematics and Computer Science of the Ural Federal University named after the first President of Russia BN Yeltsin, the Regional Educational and Scientific Center "Intelligent Systems and Information Security", the interregional public organization "Association of Heads of Information Security Services" and the Information and Communication Support Fund technologies.

RuCTF 2012 competitions were held in two stages.

The qualifying round ( RuCTF 2012 Quals ) took place on the Internet from March 16 to March 18 over 48 hours without a break.

Rules for the qualifying stage RuCTF 2012 Quals here .

At the qualifying stage of RuCTF 2012 Quals, teams were asked to solve tasks (tasks), combined in thematic categories. For the successful solution of task teams received points. The difficulty level of the task was directly related to its cost.

In addition to this qualifying stage, teams could reach the final of RuCTF 2012, showing excellent results in other CTF competitions.

The very final of RuCTF 2012 was held from April 19 to April 22 in the city of Yekaterinburg.

The program of the final consisted of several parts:

04/19/2012 - registration of participants, the opening ceremony of RuCTF 2012, technical reports and master classes.
04/20/2012 - a round table with the organizers of the final, a report on robotics, a meeting with the sponsors of the final and a discussion of issues with the jury.
04/21/2012 - the final of RuCTF 2012 (from 9-00 to 20-00).
04/22/2012 - Analysis of the tasks from the jury and awarding of participants.

Final Rules

During a game, teams may:

  • use any number of computers and network equipment not higher than the second level of the ISO OSI stack;
  • make any changes to the servers provided to them, unless it is expressly prohibited by the jury;
  • change the topology of your network segment.

During the game, teams are prohibited from:

  • conduct attacks on the jury's computers;
  • filter traffic of rival teams from jury traffic (for example, by ip-addresses);
  • generate an unreasonably large amount of traffic (flood);
  • conduct destructive attacks on the servers of opposing teams (for example, rm ‚Äìrf /);

Text for teams, April 14, 2012

The modern world is a world of money. Money permeates all areas of society. Every person thinks about money, daily, hourly. Each person can be enriched in an instant, and can overstep a side of poverty. Money is everywhere - we buy food for survival, we take loans to satisfy whims, we make investments in the hope of preserving opportunities.

Money has become so natural that nobody no longer understands what it is - money. But still there are those in the world who understand money better than others. They know how to make money and increase their wealth, they easily make multi-million transactions, they don‚Äôt notice multi-million losses. This is a corporation. During the day they earn as much money as an ordinary person earns in a lifetime. At times it seems that they are just playing with money.

This year we will play with money. The teams now are financial giants, large holdings that are fighting among themselves in the race for enrichment. No more flags. Now all you have is money, and your task is to make even more money. At any price.

But do not worry, RuCTF is still a computer security competition, and each team will have a familiar server with pre-installed services, each of which will make money. For example, one of the services will issue loans, and another one will accept deposits. Under interest, and for a certain period - all for real. These two services will allow teams to stay afloat, even if they lose absolutely all the money - customers will regularly come to them, keep deposits and take loans.

Glasses

The world of money is unsteady. Today you can be a ruble millionaire, and tomorrow there will be a default. In the world of money, hard currency is needed, and this currency is the points of the organizers. No one cares how many millions of rupees are in your safe or pocket. To rise in the eyes of rivals, you must exchange your money for points. You can do this at any time, but remember that exchange rates do not stand still. You are free to choose what is better - change the Tugrik now, after 5 minutes of yentik or, perhaps, after 20 minutes of candy wrappers.

Money

From a technical point of view, the money involved in the game is a set of bills issued by the Central Bank. Each note is represented as a text string in CSV format and looks like this:

82154800-8337-11E1-8736-16514824019B, 1000, TUG, 10,2012-04-21T11: 00: 00, AD87 ... BOI ==, Money-money

The bill consists of the following fields:

1. the unique identifier of the bill (UUID);
2. the denomination of the note (in this case 1000);
3. currency (in this case, "Tugriks");
4. the unique identifier of the owner of the bill (natural number);
5. date and time of creation of the note in sortable-format;
6. Base64 from an electronic signature made using the CentroBank's private key;
7. optional comment that does not affect the value of the signature.

For any bill, you can easily check whether it is real - the public key of the CentroBank will be available to all participants.

Transactions

Money transactions, regardless of which parties participate in it, are conducted through the Central Bank, owned by the organizers. Before the transaction, one of the parties initiates the transfer of money, referring to the other party. They agree on the amount, currency and transaction identifier.

After that, both parties access the CentroBank API via HTTP in order for it to complete the transaction. The Central Bank imposes a limit on the time difference between the parties' connections - if one of the participants is confused, the transaction may not take place.

After connecting, the sending side in the body of the POST request transfers the bills line by line. The Central Bank selects all of them that are valid, checks that they are all the right currency, sorts them by ascending nominal and takes one bill from the beginning until it collects enough money to complete the transaction. The exchange of banknotes and the delivery of the change is not made, however, all unused valid bills will be returned to the sender in the response body in the event of a successful transaction.

Technically, when conducting a transaction, CentroBank revokes the old bills of the sender, and gives the recipient new ones. Teams will be able to check if the bill has been withdrawn with the help of a special service from the organizers.

Storage

The heart of each team's infrastructure is the Storage service, which keeps all the money belonging to the team safe. All other services of the team receive and deposit money in the Vault. The storehouse, in addition to accepting, storing and issuing banknotes, also has a peculiar idea of ‚Äã‚Äãsaving - if the service starts asking for too much money in a too short period of time and, at the same time, too little money puts it back - it is likely that the store will not like it and it will not give money.

Signature

Another support service is the Signature service. He knows how to accept input data and sign it with the private key of your team. The signature is used to authenticate requests made by the team‚Äôs services.

Team Interaction

As always, you can interact with other teams within the rules. For example, you can take a loan from a richer competitor, or make a contribution to the bank of another team. At the same time, we, of course, cannot guarantee that the service of the other team will treat you honestly. Similarly, you can try to fool other people's services. Except for one case.

Credits

In order to avoid credit fraud, the organizers are forced to control the process of issuing loans. Therefore, all transactions related to loans are made not just through CentroBank, but through a special Trustee. With the exception of some details, this service works identical to the Central Bank. In particular, before taking a loan, the client and the service must agree on a transaction identifier.

The party that took the loan may come to the Trusted Person at any time until the expiration date and return the money on the loan. Note that for this, the borrower must remember the identifier of the transaction in which the loan transaction was made.

After the maturity of the loan is due, the issuing party has the right to come to the Trustee and claim their loan with interest back. Regardless of whether the borrower has returned the money to this moment, the Trustee gives the lender money, that is, it implements the credit insurance system.

It would seem that you can take loans and not return - a direct path to wealth. But each team has a Karma.

Karma

Karma reflects the level of customer confidence in the holding. If a team does not offend its customers, its karma gradually grows to the maximum. However, when a team behaves badly, its karma begins to fall. This can occur in the following cases:

  • A customer came to the service, but could not get the service. Perhaps the service was not available at all.
  • The service team acted with the client dishonestly. For example, he accepted the contribution, but did not want to return it with interest at the right time.
  • The team took a loan from another team, however, did not return it in time.

The lower a karma team has, the less often customers will turn to it, and the less money will pass through its services.

Summary

  1. Throughout the game, teams operate with money. They can earn their legal way, as well as steal from other teams.
  2. At any time, teams can exchange notes they know for points at the current rate, while reverse exchange is not possible. The remaining money at the end of the game burns out.
  3. Money is stored centrally and can circulate freely between the services of the team, however, access to a large amount at once from any service can not be obtained.
  4. For the inoperability or fraud of the services of the team penalized by the fall of their karma. The less a karma team has, the less money passes through its services.
  5. No advisories. You can not lose concentration, because the goal - to make money.

The World Programming Championship (ICPC - International Collegiate Programming Contest, held annually under the auspices of the ‚ÄúAssociation for Computing Machinery‚Äù, the Association for Computing Machinery - ACM) is held in order to grow more and more advanced generations of programmers who can solve technical problems for more high level.

The ICPC winners of past years from Russia are the developers of VKontakte, Yandex, Mail.ru, etc.

The very first team competition in sports programming under the auspices of the Computer Engineering Association (ACM) was held in the United States, at the University of Texas in the distant 1970. The current format of the World Programming Championship was adopted in 1977, when the first final was held as part of the annual ACM conference on computer science among students.

Since then, the championship competition has been held annually, and since 1989, its main organization has been the University of Baylor (Texas, USA). Also at various times such companies as Apple, AT & T and Microsoft became the sponsors of the competitions, but since 1997 and until now the general sponsor has been IBM. Moreover, the contribution of sponsors is very significant both in technical and software equipment for participants and judges, as well as in social and educational attitudes towards all participants in the championship.

From 1977 to 1989 teams of universities from the USA and Canada mainly participated in the Olympiad, other countries of the world were represented by one European regional group. However, since 1990, thanks to the proliferation of personal computers, new regional groups began to appear each year, and the geography of the competition expanded significantly. And now students from all over the world can rightfully prove their skills and capabilities at all stages of the championship each year.

The organizers of the championship in Yekaterinburg in 2014 are:

  • Ural Federal University with the support of the City Administration and the Government of the Sverdlovsk Region;
  • ACM - Computer Association;
  • IBM is the world's largest technology and information technology company;
  • SKB Kontur is a federal developer of Internet services, business software and accounting.

Only due to clear coordination and good relations between all the organizers (the number of employees and volunteers involved in organizing the final was about a thousand people, if not more) a high professional level of the World Programming Championship in Yekaterinburg was achieved.

Each finalist team and its coach were provided with hotel rooms, travel to the competition venues, free three meals a day and a rich cultural program (yes! The solution of each task is a program written and submitted to the judges (code in C / C ++ or Java), which successfully passed a series of tests on the judges' server. The team that wins the most tasks correctly in the final wins, and in the case of equality in decision making, the team that spent less time on it than the rivals. For each decision, a certain time is awarded in minutes, for an incorrect decision, penalty minutes are also awarded.

Moreover, each team has its own tactics and strategy, invented and debugged by the coach over the years of working with the team.

For example, among the three participants there is usually one very good mathematician and two strong programmers. A mathematician reads assignments, isolates those for which there are solutions at once or may appear further, and programmers convert his decisions from sketches of textual and graphic materials in a notebook into machine code and send them to the judges for verification. While one programmer is working with a computer, the second is already throwing code on the next solution of the problem. Then they change.

If the judges decide the problem without comment, then the next task is taken up. If a notification comes from the judges that the decision is wrong, then you need to look for inaccuracies in the code and in the calculations or solve another problem, and come back to this later.

On Saturday, February 16, the 3rd All-Russian Test Turing Competition was held , which was held as part of the international conference on artificial intelligence Opentalks.AI on February 14-16.



Under the cut - numbers, diagrams, the best dialogues according to the expert group.

Overall impression


Overall, the Turing Test went well. Many teams of “bot builders” showed interest, but as a result only 3 bots were represented at the competition, since only 1.5 months passed from the moment of the announcement to the start of the Test. As a result, prizes were distributed among these three participants.

The prizes were sponsored by MTS. At their booth was placed a screen on which the statistics of the Test Turing online were displayed and interesting dialogs that had previously been moderated were displayed.

“Nanosemantics Laboratory” also connected its bots, but they were not taken into account when choosing winners. These were the Blonde bots (you can continue to chat with her at this link ), Gopnik, Durov and Zarov (you can also chat with them here ).

Volunteers who played the role of robots, previously instructed how they need to conduct dialogues to confuse the interlocutors. Judging by the results, they coped well with their task. The best of them are our partners OpenTalks and MTS will also be awarded cash prizes.



Interestingly, a man easily pretends to be a bot. At first, the conference volunteers did not cope with the task, but on the second day they learned to play the role of a bot well. Some even showed ingenuity: they turned to Yandex.Alisa on a mobile phone in order to understand how best to respond.



When working with our bots, there were technical problems. As a result, a large number of dialogues did not end, but we did not take them into account about the evaluation of results. Curiously, the bots managed to pretend to be human in 58% of cases (~ 34/59%). Such a large percentage, it seems to us, is due to two reasons:

  1. In this contest, people “helped” the bots to win by playing the role of bots. In the classic Turing Test, this is not provided for by the rules: people simply communicate without pursuing any goal.
  2. Dialogue with a person or a bot was limited to 12 replicas. It is clear that with an increase in the length of the dialogue, the probability of a person recognizing the essence of the interlocutor grows.

Such restrictions made the Test less scientific, but more fun. Our past Tests (2015 and 2016) were more difficult.

Interesting observations


  • Thesis : Robots speak short and dry. Reality : Nothing like that! Bots can produce long, coherent responses.
  • Thesis : Robots are not joking. Reality : Both the neural network chit-chat bots and the bots created by the talented editors are quite funny.
  • Thesis : The robot answers all questions. That's why he is a robot. Reality : Do you want to ask a question?

Summary


The Nanosemantika company has been organizing Test Turing in Russia for the third time, and each time the overall situation with the competition changes. Participant bots are gradually becoming more sophisticated and convincing in imitating natural dialogue. They use various dialog construction algorithms, including not only rules, but also machine learning to implement chit-chat (“chat”, meaningless communication). The test shows that the computer can really imitate "free communication", although it takes a lot of time for such a program to be of high quality.

Will we do the test again? Sure to! The Turing test allows you not only to acquaint the public with the real possibilities of modern dialogue technologies available to any developer (and not necessarily work for this at Google or IBM), but also gives development teams the opportunity to measure the dynamics of the quality of their bot.

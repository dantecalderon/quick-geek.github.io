Export


At the end of 2017, the volume of exports of computer and information services exceeded $ 3.5 billion. The average annual growth rate of exports of products of the information technology industry over the past three years has been at 16%.

Russia's share in the global export of IT-services is 0.8%.

The Ministry of Communications and Mass Media notes the high positions of Russian system integrators who work in the global market. The share of Russian companies in the global IT services market is about 1.5%, and domestic companies are more concentrated in the high-tech project development sector, occupying 8 positions in the rating of 100 leading service companies in the world.

Future plans


The Ministry of Communications and Mass Media has determined the main directions for the implementation of the Strategy, taking into account the problems that are now visible (staff shortage):

  • Mass retraining of specialists.
  • Improvement of physical and mathematical education in schools.
  • Modern professional training of informatics teachers.
  • The increase in the number of students in specialized specialties.
  • The development of engineering skills in business engineering and entrepreneurship skills among students, as well as in-depth study of basic end-to-end digital technologies.
  • Stimulating the work of highly qualified industry professionals
    information technology in Russia.
  • Popularization of IT as a field of activity.
  • Improving institutional conditions.
  • International cooperation and export support.
  • R & D, including:
    • photonics;
    • new parallel computing algorithms;
    • new biometric systems;
    • development of interaction algorithms for robotic systems and humans;
    • creating human-machine interfaces;
    • introduction of new neurocognitive technologies;
    • software for augmented (modified) reality
  • Priority areas of research:
    • cloudy, foggy and dewy calculations;
    • blockchain in the storage system of medical cards, diplomas and workbooks of Russian citizens, in housing and utilities and construction;
    • Internet of Things;
    • machine learning algorithms;
    • Big Data;
    • Information technology, including the creation of a new generation of secure communication networks based on the quantum effects of encoding and transmission of information, the creation of stable and self-healing algorithms and methods;
    • new development and testing tools;
    • the creation of "digital twins" to replace the implementation of complex sets of human operations.

The strategy also provides for the support of small business, that is, preferential loans and subsidies for Russian IT customers when purchasing domestic products and implementing projects to migrate their information systems from foreign to domestic solutions.

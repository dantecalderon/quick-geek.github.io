Getting started


For the device to work correctly, you need to install the Kingston Bolt app from the App Store on your smartphone or tablet. It provides support for all functions of the gadget: backup photos from a smartphone to an external drive and photo-video with direct storage on a USB flash drive, and viewing media materials. The application interface is simple and clear - even a baby will figure out the five buttons and classic galleries.



When iOS is first launched, it will request access to the photos, after which the program will offer to make a full copy of all media files. Subsequent backups will capture only new images and videos, which significantly reduces the time to create the next backup. In addition, the application maintains separate indexes for each device used. This means that you can back up multiple phones and use the drive to save other data from your computer. Backups will be labeled according to the names of each gadget, and will not interfere with each other.




In addition to the usual backup, the program provides several other useful options. For example, you can create a backup on a flash drive by deleting the sources from the internal memory of the phone. Transferring photos and videos from a flash drive to the phone, similar to the original accessories from Apple, is also available. Also interesting is the mode of directly saving photos and videos to an external drive, bypassing the built-in memory. This option will allow you to avoid unnecessary copying procedures with the subsequent removal, and simplifying viewing of the resulting media on external devices. However, there is a much more interesting scenario. Two seasons of the best series of the last year, full-length BD-rip in 720p on the road? With a fill on the i-device, it will take a lot of time and almost all the space on your device, and even iTunes can show off. With Bolt, it's about 30-40 minutes and zero problems.

Practical testing


Unfortunately, the certification process at Apple is quite strict and for the steps to the left or the right is not shooting, but close to that. Therefore, at the moment DataTraveler Bolt is a pure photo accessories, the application does not yet provide any additional functionality. You, of course, will be able to write anything on a USB flash drive - music or other documents, a cryptocurrency wallet or some other “secret” data. That's just iOS itself with its insane DRM will not show anything. However, in the future it is possible to update the application - here the only question is the goodwill of Apple. Keyboards and browsers have already been allowed to change, perhaps, they will get to third-party drives?

In terms of immediate functionality, everything is smooth. Currently available as a full copy, and backup of only selected photos and videos, viewing them on the device and working with several iPhones in turn. Backups are not mixed up, each gadget creates its own folder in which it stores all synchronized good. To connect to a PC device uses the latest generation USB interface - 3.1 Gen 1.



Records in terms of copying speed should not be expected (however, the closeness of iOS also affects, not least the limiting performance when working with third-party devices), but for everyday use it will be quite enough. In our test, transferring nearly 6 GB of data (500 photos and 20 good videos) from iPhone 8 to Bolt took about 12 minutes, and copying from the drive to the MacBook Pro - 42 seconds.

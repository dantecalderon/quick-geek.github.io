The list of reports and brief descriptions read under the cut.

List of speakers:


Sergey Savkin

Smart House


  • On the opensource-based smart home platforms.
  • How Home-Assistant won the tech race.
  • The main protocols for the exchange of information in the field of Internet of things.
  • Can a smart home cost 3,000 rubles?
  • LoRaWAN wireless technology.



Yury Lilekov

GSM communication for DIY is easy!


  • Let's talk about the features of common GSM-modules.
  • Consider ways to connect them to different controllers.
  • Let's try to assemble the cheapest standalone GSM-IoT device with a minimum of soldering and test it a little.



Evgeny Savitsky

An open platform for assembling a smart apartment based on Bluetooth LE and open source software


I want to talk about the current state of the concept of "smart" home, in terms of solutions for "smart" apartment, as well as to offer a solution and technology that may interest the DIY-community. The purpose of this technology is to provide a simple and affordable way to start collecting your “smart” apartment and not to pay a lot of money for expensive box solutions.

Boris Rebrov

DIY carpentry workshop, expectations and reality


  • What is the carpentry workshop (history of creation)?
  • Types of work (+ types of connections).
  • The necessary tools and equipment (what you should buy, what you can do yourself).
  • Unevident difficulties.
  • The first products.



Vladislav Zaitsev

Cellular networks: tell me who your neighbor is and I will tell you who you are


  • Radio network topologies: “star”, “expanded star”, “point-to-point”, “mesh network”, “tree”.
  • Pros and cons of mesh networks. Why is this even necessary? Why not install repeaters? Where are mesh networks superior to other topologies? What are their cons?
  • Principles of networking, routing in storing mode and non-storing: store a table on each device or on the coordinator? Why do you need a routing table?
  • What you need to know to understand the conversation about cellular networks: boring terminology and boring tales.
  • "At whose expense the banquet" or about what hardware it all works. What are the power requirements of endpoints?
  • The difference between 6LoWPAN, ZigBee and Z-Wave. Protocols, standards and algorithms: closed standards and certification against open specifications and the principle of "drank what you want."
  • What can I take to play: a quick start on open technologies



Kirill Smirnov

Development of an unmanned vehicle from scratch for dummies. Part 2: stereo vision. Jetson TK1 + PlayStation EYE v2 Camera


  • What is the system for controlling the model of an unmanned vehicle.
  • What are the elements of the stereo vision subsystem.
  • Review of cameras available on the market.
  • Description of the computer Jetson TK1 and its features.
  • The story of the experience of creating this system.



Peter Blokhin

Sports minicar. From idea to protopipa


  • Introduction it will be about this kind of motorsport, like karting. A few introductory words about this.
  • Definition of goals, objectives, the formation of T.Z.
  • The complexity of the theoretical approach to development.
  • Difficulties of practical implementation.
  • Then we will discuss the components that make up a sports car; highlights and tricks of manufacture.
  • Findings.



Pavel Budnikov

Robot Tractor, a prototype agricultural robot


  • The concept: why do we need a prototype and why so big.
  • Design and manufacture of chassis: wheels or tracks, 3D design and welding of aluminum.
  • Component selection: Ublox Neo or JAVAD, Raspberry Pi and Arduino or Nvidia Jetson and CAN Bus, lidar or Intel Realsense, motors, drivers and batteries.
  • ROS and robots, or everything is thought up to us (almost).
  • Management and high-precision navigation: joystick from Xbox, PPP and RTK in RTKLib, lidar, odometer and Kalman filter, building a route and avoiding obstacles.
  • Everyday work with iron: chase through the park behind a riotous robot, “again everything burned down”, “everything needs to be redone” and other stories.



Kirill Snezhko

One app to rule everyone: a universal app for cameras with wireless interfaces


  • We understand the protocols and interfaces of some cameras (Ricoh Theta SC, Xiaomi Yi Action Camera, Xiaomi Yi Smart Dash Camera, Nikon D7500)
  • Decompilation and modification of official applications.
  • API cameras and almost everything connected with them.
  • Procurement for your own camera aggregator application.

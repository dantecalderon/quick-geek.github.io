Contrary to the widely tolerated programs of zero tolerance for road accident victims, the number of fatalities in the world continues to grow. Analysis of existing road safety concepts


Historically, the first concept was tougher penalties. Its inefficiency has become apparent long ago: even all the fines put together, plus the execution of the perpetrators, will not be able to resurrect any of those killed in an accident. And any tightening works for three years, after which the person gets used to it and everything returns to the beginning. That is, the first concept does not work at all.

By the 60s of the twentieth century, it was impossible to put up with a rapid increase in the number of dead, and automakers began to seriously improve the safety of cars (seat belts, airbags, ABS, etc.). And the cars themselves began to do, taking into account the deformation zones. In the road infrastructure appeared bump stops and separation barriers. By the nineties, it became clear that the physical limit of passive safety of cars had been reached. That is why, with the allowed speed of 110 km / h, crash tests
held at a speed of only 60 km / h. Thus, the second concept was very effective, but its possibilities are almost completely exhausted.

Realizing this, at the turn of the millennium, instead of searching for a new concept of improving transport, transport populists engaged in fraud and began to LOSS the characteristics of transport and infrastructure. It was stated that cars drive too fast and this speed should be reduced in every way by both administrative and infrastructural measures. At the forefront of this concept was Sweden, which in 1997 adopted a program known as Vision Zero. The goal was declared "zero tolerance" for deaths in road accidents, with the goal of reaching zero levels by 2020. In the year to 2020, it is clear that this program has completely failed.

If you take the majority of articles on Vision Zero, then the ‚Äúfuture growth‚Äù security schedule will end in 2010 or 2013. Why? And because after 2013 the program Vision Zero stopped working! The death toll has ceased to decline and fluctuates around 2013 values. Moreover, the forecast graph, for the statistically significant last 5 years, in Sweden gives a potential zero only by the year 2410!



But the main problem is not this, since it was still possible to achieve a serious reduction in the number of dead. The main problem is that the success of Vision Zero is greatly exaggerated, and the methods in general are frauds. Since the reduction in the number of victims of accidents is achieved not due to an increase in SAFETY, but due to the DEGRADATION of the characteristics of the transport and transport infrastructure. But this is the same as improving the safety of the saw by dulling its teeth. Yes, it will be safer, but due to the fact that it will become worse to cut. And this is the essence of all Vision Zero offers! See for yourself, all four Vision Zero directions are such frauds:

  1. Vision Zero requires to reduce the speed of transport to a safe 50 or even 30 km / h. But this is absurd! Let us then immediately reduce the speed to zero, or ban cars altogether. No cars, no dead from cars. That is, the best medicine is the guillotine. But before that (in the second concept), solutions were developed to increase safety without reducing speed.
  2. Vision Zero offers to physically spread pedestrians and vehicles, making all pedestrian crossings off-street. Yes, pedestrian safety will increase, but at the expense of reducing the convenience of pedestrians. And for older people in general fatal, since not every person can physically go up to the third floor, and then descend. And the tremendous price of extra-public transitions forced us to abandon this idea even in small and rich Sweden. However, in many countries, nodding at Vision Zero, they continue to attack the same rake.
  3. Vision Zero offers all country roads to do with the separation barrier in the middle. The idea is good, only for overtaking opportunities, all roads will have to be made in four lanes (two lanes in each direction). But it turned out to be too expensive even for small and rich Sweden. Then, using the data of the simplest motion detector, specifies the coordinates of each object in the current frame. Thus, the classical task of recognizing arbitrary three-dimensional objects on a changing three-dimensional background, unsolvable without strong artificial intelligence, we reduced to the task of not even tracking, but of refining the coordinates of objects on a fixed flat background. At the same time, the amount of information transmitted over the network is thousands of times less than with the classical video transmission to a single center.

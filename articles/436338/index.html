How does Vnukovo airport work

Vnukovo was launched on July 2, 1941, the project was supervised by K. Ye. Voroshilov. We wanted to be in time by August 18 - the Day of the Air Fleet of the USSR. But the war began on June 22, so I had to run faster, repaint the hull from white to black and green. The Moscow special-purpose air group (MAGON) made 60 thousand sorties to the front line and to the rear of the enemy. The Germans did not touch the runway themselves (it seems they expected to use it during the seizure), but they bombed the building and fuel depots, anti-aircraft installations nearby.



The airport is in the city, so the subway will lead to it. The project of the new terminal, oddly enough, is German. It was originally designed for the convenience of passengers in the first place, so he has a rather unusual usability.

Here goes a two-story aeroexpress. This is the first airport that introduced silence in Russia: announcements are only critical, all flights in succession are not credited.

But let's start gradually.

How do people get to the airport


Since Vnukovo is in the city, it can be reached by bus (stops at an underground pedestrian crossing that leads to terminal A building), by aeroexpress (arrives at a unique underground station located directly in the passenger terminal) and by car (including free to drop car-sharing car at a special parking lot).



From the subway by bus to get about 15-20 minutes. By express train from Kievsky Station - 35 minutes. Kiev highway for cars is fairly free (in comparison with other free roads to airports, but, of course, not during peak hours), you can also get to Vnukovo airport via three more multi-speed highways: Borovsky, Minsk and Kaluzhsky.


The tunnel connects the airport building, Aeroexpress and Hilton. In the transition, robots speak in a voice before the departure of the next train.


Like the entire airport, it is cleaned constantly: 7/24/365. Special services monitor the quality of cleaning and climate.


At the exit from the tunnel, the first traces of what the architect thought well begin: here these luminaires diffuse the electric light and reflect the daytime onto the escalator so that both sources are not blind.

The staff of the airport complex is about 16.5 thousand people and lives mainly in the village of Vnukovo and in Peredelkino near, in other nearby residential areas. The airport is tightly surrounded by residential buildings on three sides. There is a whole complex of everything you need near the airport: a canteen, a large private hospital with a hospital, a lot of offices. Dispatchers monitor the movement of aircraft on the platform and in the sky, they sit generally apart from the airport, in the tower on the airfield.

Opposite the airport there is a hotel, which also leads an underground passage. The main entrance to the Hilton is located opposite Terminal A, where all passenger flights are served on arrival and departure.

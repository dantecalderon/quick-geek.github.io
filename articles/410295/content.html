From junior to senior


Traditionally, let's not rush and approach the hero of this publication gradually, first highlighting the younger models of the new line.


Epson EH-TW5400




Epson EH-TW5400 - the most "budget" model series EH-TW5xxx. The projector supports 3D and, similarly to previous models, does it “to the conscience”, providing high brightness in 3D mode. The Epson EH-TW5400 has a lower maximum brightness (2500 lumens, a 200-watt lamp) and better contrast than lower-cost entry-level projectors.


Due to the lower brightness, the EH-TW5xxx series is not completely analogous to the new Epson EH-TW6xx models: even if they are close, the Epson EH-TW5400 will still give better results in a darkened room . Although to say that it is losing in brightness, it would be unfair: the whole series has a high brightness, it is quite possible to work with large screens or in a lighted room (well, in fact, 2500 lm for this model and 3000 lm for the same Epson EH -TW610 - the difference in the region is only 15%).


A diagonal of 100 inches EH-TW5400 gives from a distance of 272 cm. There is no Wi-Fi support.


Epson EH-TW5600




Epson EH-TW5600 - "standard" version. The projector has a more advanced lens with optical zoom of 1.6 × and the lens shift function vertically, for the first time in the line of budget Epson projectors , by the way!


A screen with a diagonal of 100 inches is obtained from a distance from 295 to 478 cm.


The difference in contrast with Epson EH-TW5400 is stated as minimal, i.e. if you do not need lens shift and you can place the projector directly in front of the screen, or you don’t worry about some loss of sharpness when programmatically correcting the trapezoid, then you can save money and safely take the younger model ...


... or still not stop and read further, because ...


Come on! Come on! Epson EH-TW5650



And now, we finally turn to the “highlight of the program,” the Epson EH-TW5650 projector , the “top-end model of the budget segment.” What's in it "top" and why so much "HYIP"?


First of all, we immediately get built-in support for Wi-Fi and Miracast - you can duplicate the screen of compatible mobile devices and even output side by side with HDMI. So-so addition, except for "connoisseurs" who love less wires.


The most interesting thing is this: for the Epson EH-TW5650 model , the contrast is twice as great as that of the Epson EH-TW5400 and Epson EH-TW5600 ! Since the contrast value published in the specifications refers to the so-called “dynamic contrast”, which cannot be evaluated on a single frame, the knowledgeable public can out of habit simply skip this figure, referring to the fact that, they say: “they invent the contrast values ​​again, comparing full on and full off with auto iris " .


Fairly, I do not argue. Therefore, hereinafter we will first of all talk about the so-called. “Native” contrast , i.e. one that can actually produce three LCD arrays installed in the projector without any tricks to amplify black in dark scenes, such as a dynamic aperture (which is also available on the Epson EH-TW5650).



Despite the fact that Epson EH-TW5650 was not separately positioned by the company as something unique, it stands out from the total number of our budget models by the output image. Therefore, in order not to be unfounded, and in order to make sure that it was not only this unique copy that we came across, I decided to see if the experts agree with this conclusion in their reviews.

It is very difficult to get such a high mark for the contrast from them, but the numbers speak for themselves - according to the French version, the “native” contrast is 1700: 1 (in the most accurate color rendering mode). In order to correctly compare with analogs, you can see their reviews of home models on DLP technology within 2000 Euros - the contrast, according to PHFR, will vary between 900: 1 and 1900: 1.


Just for comparison: the typical contrast of an IPS monitor is around 1000: 1.


That is what I wanted to tell you on such a large scale. The fact is that the Epson EH-TW5650 contrast ratio is indeed twice as high as the low-end models. In general, it is not at all the "budget" level , if we speak frankly.

According to the PW data, the contrast level in the economy mode of the lamp is 1164: 1 (in the most accurate mode of operation), and 1650: 1 in the maximum brightness mode. In general, projectorworld.ru and other projector manufacturers also generally get smaller numbers than projection- homecinema.fr. Perhaps the difference is in testing methods or equipment.


Immediately anticipate the question that may appear to the reader: "Why does the contrast turn out higher in the maximum brightness mode?"


Higher contrast in the maximum brightness mode is ensured due to the higher dynamic range of the resulting image, i.e. the difference between completely dark and bright scenes.



Additional margin of brightness is responsible for the expansion of DD. For example, if in a completely dark room specially prepared for DK, a film is shown with dark and bright scenes, then it turns out that dark scenes will be just dark (as they should be), and light ones , for example, on a bright sunny day , can be perceived as if the sun really blinded the eyes, especially with the rapid transition from one scene to another. Whether watching movies in this mode or still switching to economical is a matter of taste.


The author also reports that the automatic aperture of the Epson EH-TW5650 began to work silently, complementing the already good “native” contrast, which makes the gain in terms of contrast even more noticeable. This and we can confirm after a long test of the projector in the office.

Total


Summing up, I would like to note with pleasure that the latest updates of Epson home projectors' lines were every time connected with a significant expansion of their capabilities, and not just “changing the number in the model number”.

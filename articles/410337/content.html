In December 2017, a significant part of the Epson Print Factory series was updated.



Of the most notable and interesting changes:


  • the design of refueled tanks has changed - they are now frontal and tightly fixed in the device case;
  • containers themselves were updated, and, in my opinion, very successfully, so to speak, “for people” (more on this below).
  • for the first time in printers and MFPs, a device with a newest PrecisionCore print head appeared , which previously only Epson business devices could boast of.

Now a little more about the changes and, in fact, the very line of devices.


Improvement number 1. Sideways ahead


So, the capacity for refilling ink "moved" from the side of the printer - in the front. What does this give? It became more convenient to refill the printer: you can put it on the shelf and ... and that’s all. Remove and move tanks for refueling, as it was necessary to do earlier, is no longer required. Open the front cover, tilt the valve lever and refuel.



In addition, it became much easier to track the level of ink remaining on the front-mounted containers.


Well, thanks to a change in the design of the device have become much smaller. 30% interest, at least:



Improvement number 2. The system of refilling ink "Key Lock" and "Ink Lock" ink containers of a new generation - not a drop by!


Did you happen to be distracted by [substitute any event that could distract at such a crucial moment] confuse the colors of ink tanks and fill in with purple instead of cyan ink? Or do not get the tip of the container with ink in the hole and get dirty when refueling?


So, now each container with ink has its own unique shape of the nozzle , suitable only for the corresponding filling hole on the principle of a key with a lock. Do not miss and do not confuse.

Ink is no longer spilled!


As shown in the video above, the design of the valve installed in the container is designed in such a way that the ink supply is always blocked when the container is not inserted into its “groove” on the device itself (the so-called “Ink Lock” system). In fact, the open container with ink is now vertically inserted into the printer, and after refueling it is removed in the same way, and at the same time - not a drop by!

Improvement number 3. Black pigment - all without exception


In addition to what I have already mentioned, in all updated models with frontal capacities, black ink is only pigmented . What is the advantage of the pigment over the water-soluble ink, I think, it is not necessary to explain once again, so I will simply list the main ones:


  • the pigment is perfectly fixed on paper (because it is abrasive) ;
  • does not spread when water enters or is released by a marker (because it is perfectly fixed) ;
  • does not fade with time (because the pigment) .

Therefore, to print text (and especially for long-term archival storage) pigment ink is an ideal option.



What models have such improvements

If you choose a color multifunction printer “more serious”, I mean for the office , you can choose one of three new products: Epson L6160 , Epson L6170 and Epson L6190 (will be available in February — March) .



In addition to these advantages and the availability of "office" chips, such as the front tray for printing a large volume of documents, removable waste ink tank (yes, and then a pleasant surprise - you will no longer need to contact the service, you can change the diapers yourself) Ethernet connections, “six-thousand” models are equipped with the latest generation print head - Epson PrecisionCore .

Until recently, this technology was used only in professional wide-format and business printers designed for very large print volumes (for example, Epson WorkForce Pro RIPS cartridgeless printers or our incredibly fast flagship Epson WorkForce Enterprise with print speeds of up to 100 ppm).


Now productive printing heads began to be installed in printers for small offices.


This printing technology was created to level the difference in the quality of prints made on laser and inkjet printers. At the same time, Epson PrecisionCore allows you to “speed up” print speeds, retaining the color saturation inherent in inkjet printing, high fade resistance and the ability to work with different media (papers).

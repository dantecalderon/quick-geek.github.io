One of the very first Dell announcements at CES 2019 was the update of the flagship of our consumer ultrabooks. What is new? First, the engineers managed to make a webcam module so compact that in the XPS 13 9380 he was able to safely move from under the display back to the upper frame above it. Many corporate users, who often communicate with colleagues via video link, have been waiting for this.

As for the "stuffing", then the new XPS 13 processors are installed Intel Core i5-8265U and i7-8565U. Dell Latitude 7400


The second interesting novelty is an enterprise-class laptop Latitude 7400 . It has become one of the first computers in the world that support Intel Context Sensing technology. One of the ways to apply it is to issue more relevant recommendations based on user activity on the web. But another possibility is much more interesting: using sensors, the software is determined by the user or not. For example, if you sit in a chair in front of a laptop, then he will start the authentication process in the system for face recognition. And if you decide to interrupt work and go for a cup of coffee, then you should just get out of the chair, and then the system will lock itself.



In addition, the Dell Latitude 7400 is also the most compact 2-in-1 computer in our portfolio of commercial products. The 14-inch display of the gadget can be completely folded back, turning it into a tablet. At the same time, the device weighs less than 1.4 kilograms in basic configurations. The matrix here, of course, is sensory and with a resolution of 1920x1080. Full HD is chosen in order to maximize the battery life of the model. In the version with a 78W battery, the laptop can work for up to 24 hours without connecting to the mains! It supports fast charging technology.



The hardware component also looks interesting. At the heart of the entire eighth generation of updated Intel processors, called Whiskey Lake - they were already discussed above in the block about XPS 13 9380. Also inside the Latitude 7400 can be installed SSD NVMe drive with up to 2 TB and up to 16 GB of RAM.

At the ends of the gadget, there are two full-sized USB 3.0 connectors, HDMI, and two Thunderbolt 3, one of which can act as a port for charging. There is support for NFC, FIPS 201 smart card reader. And there are still versions of Latitude 7400 with LTE modem and SIM slot.

We are planning to bring the model to Russia for corporate customers in May. At the moment, the estimated starting price of basic configurations is from 108,000 rubles.

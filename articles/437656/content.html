Over time, everyone is faced with the need to produce large parts using additive technologies. And household 3D printers, in their bulk, have a relatively small table for printing (a square with a side of 180 .... 220 mm). Not much. What to do if you want more?

There are various interesting solutions for increasing print sizes, including huge expensive industrial printers, but there are also budget options for printing at home.

In any case, the presented printers will help in case you need to print a lot in every sense. The built-in print pause will help out when the coil is replaced, when power is lost (the printer will continue to print from the point of stop), as well as in the case of layer-by-layer color printing. Pre-assembled printers allow you to print "from a cold start," it just takes time to install and fix the vertical part (portal), as well as the connection of electronics. It is recommended to level the table before printing. Models with adhesive coating is preferable, since the edges stick off is especially important when printing large models. In this regard, Geeetech A30 looks great with special tempered glass.
